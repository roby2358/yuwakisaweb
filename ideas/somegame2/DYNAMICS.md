# SIGNAL CITY — Dynamics

A solo hex-and-counters game about an outnumbered superhero team triaging
crises across a city while a hidden Mastermind's plan ticks toward zero.

## Theme

**The feeling:** Four heroes. Twelve alarms. The Signal is lit on every
rooftop and you can only land on one of them. Whatever you let burn tonight
is on you — and the thing in the shadows is using the smoke as cover.

Not "save the world." *Choose what you don't save.*

## Key Drivers

1. **Scarcity of Agency.** Four heroes, six to ten active Crises at any
   moment, one Doom Clock that does not pause. Every turn is triage.
2. **Guardianship.** Heroes are named and have one signature Power that
   no other hero on the roster can replicate. Districts have names too —
   the Harbor, the Skyway, the Slums. When a District falls, the heroes
   who lived there lose their home terrain bonus permanently.
3. **Escalating Commitment.** The Mastermind is hidden under one of the
   Districts. Every Crisis you ignore advances the Doom Clock *and* feeds
   the Mastermind's power tier. By the time you've narrowed down where
   they are, the fight you signed up for isn't the fight you get.
4. **Comedy.** Powers are drawn from a wide pool (see `superpower.txt`)
   and combine on the same hex. *Inflatable* + *Magnetism* + a *Hostage*
   crisis on a *Harbor* hex is a story whether it works or not.

## Map & Counters

- **Hex map.** Radius 5 (91 hexes), generated by `terrain.js`. Districts
  are clusters of same-terrain hexes given a name at gen time ("the
  Skyway", "the Slumlands"). 6 District types:
  - **Downtown** — dense, slows non-flying heroes (+1 MP cost), Crises
    here are mostly Hostage and Heist.
  - **Skyway** — rooftop hex layer; only Flight heroes enter, but
    movement here is free (0 MP). Crises: Heist, Disaster.
  - **Harbor** — Aquatic and Water powers double-act here.
  - **Industrial** — Fire and Disaster crises spawn at 2x rate; Heat &
    Light powers resolve them in one tap.
  - **Underground** — fog-of-war on adjacent hexes; Night Vision and
    Echo-location see through it.
  - **Park** — civilians shelter here; falls hardest when ruined.
- **Counters:**
  - **Heroes** (4) — colored discs, palette generated per-game by
    `colortheory.js` (split-complementary so allies are visually
    distinct from threats).
  - **Crises** — small square tiles with a category icon (Fire,
    Hostage, Monster, Heist, Disaster) and a difficulty pip (1–3).
  - **Lieutenants** (3) — named villain counters that patrol.
  - **Mastermind** — face-down tile placed under a random District.
  - **Doom Clock** — track on the side, 12 ticks. Visualized as a
    `colortheory.gradient` from cool to hellfire-red.
  - **Ruined Hex marker** — placed when a District falls.

## Key Mechanics (one per driver)

### 1. The Signal — Triage (Scarcity of Agency)
Each turn the Signal places 1d3 new Crisis counters on random hexes,
weighted by terrain. Each Crisis has a difficulty (1–3). At end of
turn, every Crisis still on the board ticks the Doom Clock by its
difficulty *minus* the number of heroes adjacent to it that turn (min 0).

There is no way to "clear the queue." There are always more alarms
than heroes. The decision is *which fire to walk past*.

### 2. Signature Powers — Rule-Breaking Exceptions (Guardianship)
At game start, draw 4 Heroes from a deck. Each Hero has:
- A **Name** ("Hexline", "The Inkblot", "Dr. Fathom", "Strut").
- A **Signature Power** drawn from `superpower.txt` taxonomy. The
  Signature is implemented as a *rule exception* — it bends one core
  rule of the game, not a stat boost. Examples:
  - *Stretch* — can resolve a Crisis up to 2 hexes away without moving.
  - *Intangibility* — ignores Downtown's +1 MP cost; can pass through
    Ruined hexes.
  - *Telepathy / Read Thoughts* — when entering a District, peeks at
    whether the Mastermind is hidden under it.
  - *Pyrokinesis* — auto-resolves any Fire Crisis in same hex on entry,
    but ticks Doom +1 (collateral damage).
  - *Shrink* — costs 0 MP to enter occupied enemy hexes.
  - *Echo-location* — Underground fog does not apply.
  - *Affect Probability* — once per game, reroll the Signal phase.
- A **Flaw** — a second rule exception that hurts. *Inflatable* cannot
  enter Skyway. *Hand of Doom* destroys the Crisis *and* the District
  it stood on.

If a hero's home District (rolled at draft) is ever Ruined, that hero
loses their Signature Power for the rest of the game. **This is the
guardianship hook.** You will catch yourself defending a single block
of warehouses because losing it means losing *Hexline* with it.

### 3. The Doom Clock & Mastermind Reveal (Escalating Commitment)
The Mastermind starts at Power Tier *Near Human*. Every full Doom Clock
tick of 3 promotes them one tier on the `superpower.txt` ladder:
*Near Human → Superhuman → Planetary → Galactic → Cosmic*. Tier
determines how many Signature Powers the Mastermind has stolen from
the deck (not from your active heroes — from the *unused* draft pile,
so the player can sometimes guess what's coming).

To win, the heroes must (a) reveal the Mastermind's hex, and (b)
defeat them in combat. To reveal, you either:
- Defeat all 3 Lieutenants (each kill eliminates one District as a
  hiding place, like Clue), or
- Use a *Telepathy*, *Precognition*, or *X-Ray Vision* hero to peek.

Defeating the Mastermind requires a number of adjacent heroes equal
to their current Tier. By the time you've found them, you may not
have enough heroes left.

You lose if Doom Clock hits 12, or 4 Districts fall, or all heroes
are KO'd.

### 4. Power Collisions (Comedy)
When two heroes are on adjacent hexes and one acts, the other's
Signature Power *checks for trigger*. A short table of interactions:
- *Magnetism* + *Organic Steel* — the steel hero is yanked one hex
  toward the magnet hero (free repositioning, sometimes into a fire).
- *Self-Replication* + *Mind Control* — the controller temporarily
  gains an extra action this turn.
- *Stretch* + *Intangibility* — Stretch can reach through walls.
- *Shrink* + *Ingestion* — eats the Crisis. No, really. It just
  ends. Doom does not tick. This happens maybe once a game and it
  is the moment players will retell.

Collisions are **emergent**, not scripted: every Power has a tag
(force, mass, mind, matter, reach, sense), and any two adjacent
heroes whose tags match a small interaction table fire the bonus.
~12 interactions, ~30 Powers, ~360 possible combinations.

## Secondary Mechanics (each weaves into a key one)

- **Lieutenants patrol** with full A* to the nearest unrevealed
  Crisis (they're *helping* the Mastermind by stoking chaos). Killing
  one is the main way to narrow the Mastermind's hiding place —
  weaving Lieutenant combat into the *Reveal* mechanic. (Driver: 3.)
- **Civilians on Park hexes** count as "extra heroes" for the
  adjacent-Crisis math but can be hurt. Loss-aversion lever inside
  the triage system. (Driver: 2.)
- **The Bat-Signal hex** (one named hex at game start, picked by
  the player) lets any hero ending their turn there draw a one-shot
  *Backup Power* tile from the unused deck. Tempts heroes to leave
  the field — accumulation against scarcity. (Driver: 1.)
- **Color-coded threat overlay.** When the player hovers a hex,
  `colortheory.js` paints adjacent hexes on a gradient: green
  (heroes can resolve this turn) → yellow (next turn) → red (will
  fall). This is the *Readable Consequences* lever — every triage
  decision has a visible cost. (Driver: 1.)
- **Ruined Districts** become impassable to non-Intangible heroes
  but the *Mastermind can still hide there*. Falling a District is
  not just loss — it's loss *plus* a hiding place you can no longer
  search. (Drivers: 2, 3.)

## State Model (must fit in a struct)

```
state = {
  turn: int,
  doomClock: int,            // 0..12
  heroes: [                  // 4 entries
    { id, name, hex, hp, signature: {id, tags}, flaw, homeDistrict, poweredOut: bool }
  ],
  crises: [                  // dynamic
    { id, hex, category, difficulty, age }
  ],
  lieutenants: [             // 3 entries until killed
    { id, name, hex, alive, hidesDistrict }   // hidesDistrict ruled out on death
  ],
  mastermind: {
    hex,                     // hidden until revealed
    revealed: bool,
    tier: 0..4,
    stolenPowers: [Signature]
  },
  map: hexGrid,              // each hex: terrain, district, ruined, fog
  districts: [
    { name, hexes, fallen: bool }
  ],
  signalHex: hexId,          // the player's Bat-Signal
  rng: Rando
}
```

Every mechanic above mutates a named field. Anything that doesn't
fit here gets cut.

## Content Templates (template, don't snowflake)

Three Power templates cover everything in `superpower.txt`:

1. **Passive Exception** — modifies a movement/visibility/cost rule.
   Params: `{ rule, modifier }`. (e.g. Intangibility, Echo-location.)
2. **Activated Resolver** — on entering a hex with a matching Crisis
   tag, auto-resolve. Params: `{ tags, sideEffect }`.
   (e.g. Pyrokinesis resolves Fire, side-effect Doom +1.)
3. **Once-Per-Game** — bends a global rule for one action.
   Params: `{ trigger, effect }`. (e.g. Affect Probability rerolls.)

Crises are 5 categories × 3 difficulties = 15 parameter sets, one
resolver function per category. Lieutenants are a parameter set on
the Hero template with `controlledByAI: true`.

## Strategies

### Early Game
- **Pick a quadrant.** With 4 heroes you cannot patrol the whole
  map. Sacrifice one District type *now* and decide where to stand.
- **Spend turn 1 on the Signal.** Drawing a Backup Power early
  has compound returns. But it costs a hero-turn while alarms tick.

### Mid Game
- **Lieutenant hunt vs. Crisis triage.** Killing a Lieutenant is
  the only way to make progress on the Reveal, but it takes 2–3
  hero-turns. Doom ticks the whole time. The recurring tension:
  *invest in winning, or invest in not losing.*
- **Power collisions become real.** Once the player learns a few
  combos, positioning two heroes for synergy is more efficient than
  splitting. But splitting is the only way to cover Crises. Tension.

### Late Game
- **Fewer heroes, bigger Mastermind.** Most runs reach the reveal
  with 2 heroes still standing facing a Tier-3 Mastermind that
  needs 3 adjacent heroes to defeat. Near-miss by design. The win
  is using the Bat-Signal hex's Backup Power tile to count as a
  fourth body, or using Self-Replication, or sacrificing a Civilian.

### Anti-Strategies (must be prevented mechanically)
- **Turtling on the Bat-Signal.** Prevented because Crises spawn
  weighted to terrain, and the Signal hex doesn't suppress them.
  Doom Clock ticks regardless.
- **Ignoring the Mastermind, just resolving Crises forever.** The
  Mastermind's Tier-up triggers are *also* tied to Districts fallen
  + raw turn count, so a "perfect triage" run still loses around
  turn 18 to a Cosmic-tier reveal that no one can punch.
- **Cheesing with one super-power.** Each Signature Power has a
  hard counter terrain (Pyrokinesis is wasted in the Harbor;
  Aquatic is wasted in Skyway). The 4-hero draft prevents mono-
  power exploits because the deck is shuffled.
- **Save-scumming the Mastermind hex by peeking.** Telepathy
  Reveal costs 1 hero-turn per peek and only checks one District
  at a time — slower than killing Lieutenants on average.

## Color & Effects (using `colortheory.js`)

- **Per-game palette.** At game start, roll a `randomScheme()`
  for the city. Districts get the 5 sorted-by-luminance colors —
  Park is the lightest, Underground is the darkest, Skyway is the
  most saturated.
- **Hero counters** use the *complementary* palette of the city:
  guaranteed visual separation from terrain.
- **Threat overlay** (the green→red gradient on hover) is a
  cached `ColorTheory` colormap with N=64.
- **Doom Clock track** is a 12-band `bands()` from the city's
  palette into hellfire red — visible at all times, the same color
  language as the map so the player feels the city *being*
  consumed.
- **Power Collision flash** — when two adjacent heroes' tags match,
  the shared edge of their hexes pulses on the *complementary*
  hue of both heroes' counter colors for ~200ms. Comedy needs a
  visual punctuation mark.

## Implementation Status (MVP shipped)

**Wired up:**
- Circular radius-6 hex map; 6 named Districts via voronoi-style seeds
- 4 randomly-drafted Heroes from a 10-power deck, with home Districts
- Per-hero MP (3) and HP; click-to-select, BFS reachable highlight
- Crisis spawning weighted by District; 5 categories with glyphs and difficulty pips
- End-of-turn Doom resolution: `tick = max(0, difficulty - adjacent_heroes)`
- Doom Clock with `colortheory.gradient` cool-to-hellfire visualization
- Mastermind tier promotion every 3 Doom; tier name from `superpower.txt` ladder
- 3 Lieutenants patrolling toward nearest Crisis (greedy 1-hex step)
- Lieutenant kills rule out hidden Districts; last-survivor reveals Mastermind
- Telepathy on-entry District peek (alternate reveal path)
- Win: enough adjacent heroes (= tier+1) on the revealed Mastermind hex
- Lose: Doom 12 / 4 Districts fallen / all heroes KO'd
- District fall when half its hexes are ruined; home-District fall strips a hero's Power
- Crisis flash animation on resolution (`requestAnimationFrame` driven)
- Per-game palette via `ColorTheory.randomScheme`; complementary scheme for hero counters

## Open Questions

- Should KO'd heroes return at the Bat-Signal after N turns? Lean
  *no* — permanent loss feeds Guardianship harder. But test it.
- Mastermind reveal: should the *final* fight be a separate scene
  or just a hex combat? Lean hex combat — keep one rules language.
- Civilian counters: cap at 6? More creates micro-management.
