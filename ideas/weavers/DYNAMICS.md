# Weavers of Worlds: The Loom

## Theme

The exhilaration of cosmic creation under pressure. You are an ensemble of cosmic artisans — Seers, Alchemists, Oracles, Wanderers, Dreamers, and Guardians — weaving new worlds into existence on the Loom of Creation before entropy tears the fabric of reality apart. The feeling is collaborative craftsmanship against the clock: threads trickle in, worlds burst into being, and the Unraveling creeps ever closer.

## Key Drivers

1. **Accumulation and Windfall** — Threads trickle in one per Weaver per turn. Then suddenly you have enough for a World, Resonance cascades bonus threads across the map, and the momentum shifts. The baseline is patient gathering; the ceiling is chain-weaving multiple Worlds in a windfall turn.

2. **Guardianship** — Each of the 6 Weavers is irreplaceable. The Seer reveals hidden Patterns, the Guardian cleanses the Unraveling, the Dreamer generates free threads. Losing access to any ability (by having their terrain consumed) permanently reduces your options.

3. **Scarcity of Agency** — 6 Weavers with 2 MP each (Wanderer gets 4). The Unraveling spreads from multiple Rifts simultaneously. You can't gather everywhere, weave everywhere, and defend everywhere. Every turn is triage: which hex do you abandon?

## Secondary Drivers

- **Readable Consequences** — Unraveling spreads visibly. Thread counts always shown. Reachable hexes highlighted. The player traces every outcome.
- **Escalating Commitment** — Each Rift adds permanent pressure. Late game has 4-5 Rifts spreading simultaneously. The final Worlds are a desperate sprint.
- **Variable Reinforcement** — Pattern discoveries (hidden bonuses on the map), Aether terrain (gives rarest thread), and Rift placement add variance. The Resonance Echo pattern re-triggers all existing World bonuses — a massive windfall if timed right.

## Key Mechanics

### 1. Thread Gathering (Accumulation)
Each Weaver standing on a thread-producing hex collects 1 thread of that type at dawn. Predictable, satisfying, the heartbeat of the game. Driver: accumulation.

### 2. World Weaving (Windfall)
Spend threads matching a recipe to place a World counter on a Weaver's hex. The World permanently blocks Unraveling on that hex. Resonance: all hexes within 2 produce bonus threads next dawn. If adjacent to another World, Harmony doubles the bonus range. Driver: accumulation and windfall.

### 3. The Unraveling (Escalating Commitment)
Rifts appear at map edges starting turn 3, one every 4 turns. Each Rift spreads Unraveling to 1 adjacent hex per turn. Unraveled hexes stop producing threads and cost +1 MP to traverse. Completed Worlds block Unraveling permanently. If the Nexus (center hex) is consumed, the Loom is lost. Driver: escalating commitment, scarcity of agency.

### 4. Weaver Abilities (Roles Through Mechanical Exceptions)
Each Weaver breaks one rule:
- **Seer**: Reveals hidden Patterns within 3 hexes on arrival. (Information as currency)
- **Alchemist**: Converts any 1 thread to any other, once per turn. (Flexibility)
- **Oracle**: Automatically deflects 1 Unraveling spread within 2 hexes per turn. (Defense)
- **Wanderer**: 4 MP instead of 2, terrain costs reduced by 1 (min 1). (Exploration)
- **Dreamer**: Generates 1 free Dream thread each dawn regardless of position. (Accumulation)
- **Guardian**: Cleanses 1 adjacent Unraveled hex each dawn. (Protection)

### 5. Patterns (Variable Reinforcement)
7 hidden Pattern tokens scattered across the map. Walking onto one activates it. Types: Thread Cache (+2 random threads), Ancient Loom (next World costs -1 thread), Starmap (reveals all Patterns), Resonance Echo (all Worlds resonate again), Weaver's Rest (all Weavers +2 MP).

## Turn Structure

1. **Dawn** (auto) — Weavers gather threads from their hex terrain. Abilities trigger (Dreamer, Guardian). Resonance bonuses applied.
2. **Move** (interactive) — Select Weavers and move them. Each has individual MP. Seer reveals Patterns. Stepping on Patterns activates them.
3. **Weave** (interactive) — Select a Weaver, click an affordable recipe to place a World at their hex.
4. **Dusk** (auto) — Rifts may spawn. Unraveling spreads. Oracle deflects. Win/lose check.

## Strategies

### Early Game (Turns 1-4)
Spread Weavers to diverse terrain types. Prioritize Wellsprings and Nebulae (cheap terrain, produce Light and Dream — the most-used thread types). Send Wanderer exploring for Patterns. Keep Guardian near Nexus.

### Mid Game (Turns 5-10)
First Rifts appear. Start weaving cheap Worlds (Dawnworld: 2 Light + 1 Star) to establish Resonance chains. Position Worlds to block predicted Unraveling paths. Use Alchemist transmute to fill gaps in thread inventory.

### Late Game (Turns 11+)
Multiple Rifts spreading. Race to weave remaining Worlds. Guardian and Oracle on Unraveling duty. Exploit Harmony (adjacent Worlds) for massive Resonance turns. Pray for Resonance Echo pattern.

### Anti-Strategies
- **Turtling at Nexus**: Only Nexus terrain produces no threads. Staying home = no gathering = no Worlds = loss.
- **Ignoring Unraveling**: Unraveled hexes lose thread production and cost more MP. Unchecked, it starves you.
- **Spreading too thin**: Weavers isolated on the map edge can't reach the Nexus if Unraveling cuts them off. Keep rough formation.

## Win Condition
Weave 7 Worlds. The Tapestry is complete.

## Lose Condition
The Nexus (center hex) is consumed by the Unraveling. The Loom is lost.
