# Study 01 — Organon of Motives

*Organon: enumeration. The question "why do people play?" is answered here 120 ways.
The rule of the list: include everything — noble, shameful, trivial, dark, absurd.
Redundancy is tolerated; omission is not. Family headings are provisional handles,
not a theory.*

---

## Family A — The Body (somatic, visceral)

1. **Arousal tuning** — play as a thermostat for excitement: bored people play to warm up, anxious people play to cool down.
2. **Kinesthetic relish** — the sheer pleasure of a well-timed jump, a clean flick, a perfect parry; the body enjoying its own precision.
3. **Juice hunger** — screenshake, chimes, particles, the crunch of a hit landing; games as slot machines of sensory reward.
4. **Adrenaline on a leash** — fear and thrill with a safety word; the rollercoaster motive.
5. **The fidget** — hands need something to do; play as sanctioned restlessness (solitaire, match-3, worry beads with rules).
6. **Rhythm entrainment** — falling into a beat, a cadence of actions; play as dance you can win.
7. **Exhaustion by proxy** — the pleasant tiredness after intense play, sought like the ache after a run.
8. **Sensory bath** — soaking in a world's light, sound, and texture; play as immersion tank.
9. **The startle economy** — jump scares, close calls, near misses; the body's alarm system enjoyed recreationally.
10. **Idle-hands displacement** — playing to keep the hands from the fridge, the phone, the cigarette; play as the least-bad compulsion.

## Family B — The Hungry Mind (cognitive, epistemic)

11. **Curiosity proper** — *what is behind that door?* The oldest hook: information gaps demand closure.
12. **The learning gradient** — fun as the felt sensation of getting better; the brain rewarding digestible novelty (Koster's thesis).
13. **Pattern gluttony** — spotting, matching, and completing patterns for its own sake.
14. **System disassembly** — the urge to take the machine apart: find the rules under the rules, the seams, the exploits.
15. **Hypothesis play** — *what happens if I…?* Games as laboratories where experiments are cheap and repeatable.
16. **The optimization itch** — shaving seconds, tightening builds, min-maxing; the pleasure of the local maximum climbing.
17. **Puzzle hunger** — a posed problem is an irritant, like a pebble in the shoe of the mind; solving is relief.
18. **Model completion** — the discomfort of a half-understood system; playing until the mental map has no blank regions.
19. **Strategic depth-diving** — the joy of thinking three moves ahead and being *right*.
20. **The epiphany chase** — that instant the trick of a puzzle inverts and becomes obvious; players farm this feeling.
21. **Vicarious omniscience** — seeing the whole board, the whole map, the whole economy — a god's-eye view life never grants.
22. **Rule connoisseurship** — appreciating elegant rules the way others appreciate wine; play as tasting.

## Family C — The Will (agency, competence, power)

23. **Effort-outcome legibility** — in games, trying harder reliably works; play as refuge from a world where it often doesn't.
24. **Competence proof** — demonstrating to *yourself* that you are capable; the private trophy shelf.
25. **The number goes up** — visible, monotonic progress; a life where growth is guaranteed and graphed.
26. **Consequence-free power** — command armies, spend fortunes, burn cities; potency without liability.
27. **Control hunger** — a bounded world that does exactly what its rules say; play as antidote to chaos.
28. **Mastery's plateau-break** — the addictive cycle of wall → grind → breakthrough.
29. **Authorship** — building, decorating, naming, arranging; leaving a mark on a world that keeps it.
30. **Domination** — beating someone. Plainly. The motive polite theories euphemize.
31. **Redemption arcs** — losing, then returning to win; play as a machine for manufacturing comebacks.
32. **The clutch** — performing under pressure and *delivering*; self-respect distilled to a moment.
33. **Vicarious competence** — even watching your character be graceful confers grace; the avatar's skill is lent to you.
34. **Freedom within fences** — total liberty inside strict limits; the paradoxical agency of the sonnet.

## Family D — The Weather Inside (emotional regulation)

35. **Escape** — the front door out of a bad day; not noble, universally practiced.
36. **Distraction from pain** — grief, breakup, diagnosis; play as anesthetic with a dosage dial.
37. **Mood repair** — twenty minutes of wins to re-inflate a punctured day.
38. **Boredom relief** — the null motive, and the most common one; play as the default filler of unstructured time.
39. **Sanctioned rage** — a place to be furious where fury is expected and priced in.
40. **Safe sadness** — games that let you cry on purpose; catharsis on demand.
41. **Anxiety displacement** — swapping unmanageable real worries for manageable fake ones.
42. **The worry-sized problem** — a game hands you a problem exactly small enough to solve; relief by substitution.
43. **Emotional rehearsal** — feeling loss, betrayal, triumph in miniature before life serves the full portion.
44. **Numbness welcomed** — sometimes the goal is to feel *nothing*; grinding as self-administered static.
45. **Permission to feel** — some people can only access awe, tenderness, or grief through fiction; the game is the key.

## Family E — The Others (social)

46. **Belonging** — the guild, the clan, the regular Tuesday group; play as membership dues paid in fun.
47. **The third place** — not home, not work; games as the tavern that fits in a pocket.
48. **Status ladders** — rank, elo, leaderboards; legible social standing in a world of illegible hierarchies.
49. **Showing off** — an audience for your excellence; play as stage.
50. **Cooperative intimacy** — trusting a stranger to heal you; the fast-forwarded friendship of shared stakes.
51. **The gift of attention** — playing *with* someone is giving them hours; play as love language.
52. **Rivalry as relationship** — a good opponent is a kind of friend; the bond of the worthy adversary.
53. **Mentorship** — teaching the new player; competence converted into generosity.
54. **Shared canon** — memes, references, war stories; play as the raw material of belonging-through-lore.
55. **Social alibi** — the game gives shy people a script; you don't have to know what to say when there's a boss to fight.
56. **Courtship** — play as display and as proximity; the oldest use of games (see: every animal).
57. **Being needed** — the tank who must show up or eleven people can't raid; obligation experienced as importance.
58. **Spectacle communion** — watching together, cheering together; play as sport, sport as church.

## Family F — The Masks (identity, narrative)

59. **Trying on selves** — the merciful villain, the brave coward; identities test-driven at no cost.
60. **Wish fulfillment** — being, for an hour, what you'll never be: fast, feared, beautiful, dead-eyed calm.
61. **Story hunger** — humans are narrative-eating animals; games are stories you *chew*.
62. **Protagonism** — in life you are a minor character in seven billion stories; in the game, the camera follows *you*.
63. **Moral tourism** — visiting choices you'd never make; ethics as a landscape to hike.
64. **Self-narrative** — "I'm a gamer," "I'm a Soulsborne player," "I main support"; play as identity brickwork.
65. **The reset fantasy** — new run, new character, new save; the lure of the clean slate life doesn't offer.
66. **Continuity of self** — returning to the same save is returning to a *self* you left mid-sentence.
67. **Legacy in miniature** — hall-of-fame runs, hardcore graves, speedrun records; small immortalities.
68. **Anonymity's holiday** — being no one in particular; the mask that hides rather than displays.

## Family G — The Abyss (existential)

69. **Legible cosmos** — a world with knowable rules and fair outcomes; play as theological comfort.
70. **Meaning in bounds** — a game supplies purpose the way a frame supplies a painting; players borrow its "why" when life's is missing.
71. **Death rehearsal** — dying a thousand cheap deaths; finitude domesticated.
72. **Flow** — self forgotten, time dissolved; play as the most reliable door to the state mystics fast for.
73. **Ritual** — the daily login, the opening moves, the pre-match routine; play as liturgy.
74. **Finished things** — life completes nothing; a game *ends*, and you were there for all of it.
75. **The tended garden** — worlds that need you; play as stewardship, stewardship as meaning.
76. **Contact with the designer** — feeling a mind on the other side of the level; play as correspondence with an unseen author.
77. **Cosmic scale-play** — commanding galaxies, then rain on one pixel-farm; play as instrument for feeling *sized*.
78. **Rebellion against usefulness** — playing precisely because it produces nothing; the last uncolonized act.

## Family H — The Hooks (structural, economic, dark)

79. **Variable-ratio reinforcement** — the gambler's schedule; loot boxes and crits pay out like slot machines because they *are* slot machines.
80. **Sunk cost** — 400 hours in; leaving now would mean the hours were spent, not invested.
81. **Streaks** — the 90-day chain you dare not break; loss aversion wearing a calendar.
82. **FOMO** — limited events, rotating shops, battle passes; scarcity theater.
83. **The Zeigarnik itch** — unfinished tasks nag; games manufacture unfinishedness industrially (quest logs are itch-farms).
84. **Habit's groove** — playing because it's 9pm and 9pm is when you play; motive long since evaporated, behavior remains.
85. **Escalating commitment** — each purchase justifies the next; the collection defends itself.
86. **Social hostage-taking** — you'd quit, but the guild needs you Thursday.
87. **The almost-win** — near misses fire reward circuitry almost like wins; games tune for heartbreak-adjacency.
88. **Endowed progress** — the punch card that comes pre-stamped twice; start a bar filling and the bar demands completion.

## Family I — The Eye (aesthetic)

89. **Beauty, plainly** — some games are simply beautiful, and people go where beauty is.
90. **Elegance appreciation** — a ruleset with nothing extra; the aesthetics of mathematics worn casually.
91. **Atmosphere bathing** — fog, neon, birdsong; place as pleasure independent of goal.
92. **The gallery walk** — playing slowly to look at things; screenshot pilgrims.
93. **Sublime dread** — vast ruins, deep water, old gods; awe sought on purpose.
94. **Style as content** — playing *stylishly* when playing efficiently would win faster; grace over gain.
95. **Craft admiration** — noticing the animation's weight, the sound design's restraint; play as studying a master's brushwork.
96. **Cozy aesthetics** — smallness, warmth, rain outside the window; the game as hearth.

## Family J — The Animal (biological, instinctual)

97. **Practice theory** — play as evolution's training montage: young mammals rehearse hunting, fleeing, fighting, allying.
98. **Surplus energy** — the zoomies hypothesis; vitality overflowing its container.
99. **The hunt sublimated** — tracking, stalking, ambush, kill — the paleolithic loop, re-skinned ten thousand ways.
100. **Foraging sublimated** — gathering, hoarding, sorting berries into stacks of 64.
101. **Territory** — claiming, marking, defending; from board-game area control to spawn-camping.
102. **The chase reflex** — moving things demand pursuit; ask any cat, or any player seeing a fleeing enemy.
103. **Nesting** — base-building, inventory arranging, home decorating; the burrow instinct with a UI.
104. **Play signals** — the play-bow, the grin, "gg"; metacommunication that says *this combat is not combat* (Bateson).
105. **Neoteny's license** — humans are the ape that never stops being a cub; play persists because we never finish growing up.

## Family K — The Clock (situational, temporal)

106. **The waiting room** — play as the universal solvent of dead time: queues, commutes, insomnia's 3am.
107. **The decompression airlock** — the ritual half-hour between work-self and home-self.
108. **Sanctioned procrastination** — playing *instead of*; the game's appeal is half borrowed from the avoided task.
109. **Time made textured** — empty hours are a smooth wall; a game carves handholds into them.
110. **The appointment** — raid night, chess club Thursday; play as the skeleton that gives a week its shape.
111. **Seasonal return** — the winter game, the summer game; play keyed to weather and holiday like harvest festivals.
112. **The convalescent's companion** — sick days, recoveries, quarantines; play at the pace of a body that can do nothing else.

## Family L — The Ledger (acquisitive, completionist)

113. **Collection hunger** — the set must be completed; the gap in the row is a wound.
114. **Hoarding joy** — 4,000 potions never drunk; wealth as security blanket.
115. **The checklist trance** — ticking boxes as self-soothing; achievement lists as rosaries.
116. **Cartographic compulsion** — clearing the fog from every corner of the map; ignorance as untidiness.
117. **100% as covenant** — the completionist's vow; finishing *utterly* as a form of respect (or captivity).
118. **Trophy externalization** — achievements as memory prosthetics: proof to your future self that you were here and did this.
119. **The last one** — one more turn, one more level, one more box; acquisition's local anesthetic against stopping.
120. **Inventory as autobiography** — the save file as diary; every item a place you've been.

## First observations (pre-synthesis)

- The families are not rivals; a single session of a single game can draw on eight of them.
- Families **H (Hooks)** and **D (Weather)** answer "why keep playing" far more than "why start."
  Families **B (Mind)** and **I (Eye)** answer "why start" more than "why stay."
- Family **C (Will)** — especially #23, *effort-outcome legibility* — keeps feeling like a
  load-bearing wall. Flag for synthesis.
- The darkest motives (H) are counterfeits of the brightest ones: streaks counterfeit ritual (#73),
  FOMO counterfeits the appointment (#110), sunk cost counterfeits continuity of self (#66).
  **Every hook is a forged version of a real reason.** Flag for synthesis — this may be the
  sharpest design heuristic in the whole study.
