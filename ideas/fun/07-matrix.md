# Study 07 — The Great Matrix

*Organon: combination. Lists are crossed against lists; most cells are dull, some spark.
Method: build the grid, then harvest only the sparking cells — exhaustiveness in the
crossing, selectivity in the reporting.*

---

## Matrix I — Motive Families × Temporal Phases

Columns are the career phases from Study 06: **START** (why begin), **STAY** (why
continue), **RETURN** (why come back), **QUIT** (why leave), **REMEMBER** (what remains).
Rows are the twelve families from Study 01. Sixty cells; the sparks:

### Harvested cells

**[Body × RETURN] — The body remembers what the mind forgets.** Lapsed players' hands
still know the combos; muscle memory outlives motive memory by years. Returning players
should be given an early moment where their hands surprise them — instant re-endowment,
the opposite of the insulting returner-tutorial.

**[Mind × QUIT] — Curiosity is the only motive that quits *satisfied*.** Every other
family quits from deficit (boredom, betrayal, exhaustion); the Hungry Mind quits from
*sufficiency* — "I see how it works now." Purely cognitive games (puzzlers) thus have
short, happy careers, and adding retention hooks to them reads as grotesque faster than
in any other genre (nobody streaks a crossword... except Wordle — which did it by making
the streak *testimony*, an Others-family graft. Sparks the next cell:)

**[Others × STAY] — Grafting is the master retention move.** Games retain not by
feeding the starting motive but by *transplanting* new families onto it. Wordle grafted
social testimony onto puzzle-curiosity; MMOs graft belonging onto power fantasy;
speedrunning grafts community onto exhausted mastery. **Longevity = successful serial
transplantation.** The corollary from Study 06 stands: without a graft, every game's
career is as short as its steepest gradient.

**[Weather × START] — Nobody downloads a game while happy.** Overstated, but the kernel
holds: away-motives (D1) dominate *acquisition* — boredom, stress, the bad week — while
toward-motives dominate *recommendation* ("you have to see this"). Store pages sell to
the suffering; word of mouth sells to the curious. Two marketing channels, two
psychologies, rarely distinguished.

**[Will × RETURN] — The unfinished fight.** Rage-quits produce more returns than bored
quits. A loss that *stings* is an open loop (#83) with self-image inside it; the player
comes back to close the loop about *themselves*, not the game (P2). The most loyal
players often have the angriest exits. (Corollary: the truly dangerous churn is calm.)

**[Hooks × REMEMBER] — Hooks leave no memories.** Ask a player their favorite gaming
memory: it is never "the day I completed the battle pass." Streaks, dailies, and FOMO
occupy thousands of hours and leave *nothing* in autobiographical memory — the hours
were consumed, not lived. This is the cleanest empirical test separating counterfeit
motives from real ones (Study 01's closing thesis): **real motives leave stories;
counterfeits leave only elapsed time.**

**[Abyss × STAY] — Meaning is the cheapest content.** Players stay years for a role
(#57 being-needed, #75 stewardship) that costs the designer nothing to create.
A guild rank, a mentor badge, a corner of the world that is *yours to tend* — these
retain like a thousand hours of content at one ten-thousandth the production cost.
Underexploited everywhere.

**[Masks × QUIT] — Players quit games that trap them in an old self.** The reset
fantasy (#65) works in reverse: when a player has changed (new skill level, new taste,
new life stage) but their account/build/reputation embodies who they were, the mismatch
chafes until they leave — often to *the same game on a new account*. "Fresh start"
churn is identity molting, not dissatisfaction, and win-back campaigns misread it.

**[Clock × STAY] — The schedule is the game.** For appointment players (#110), the
game occupies a *slot*, and the slot's survival matters more than the game's quality.
Threats to the slot (patch downtime, a raid moved to Wednesday) churn these players
more efficiently than bad content ever could. Conversely a game that *builds* the slot
(weekly reset rhythms) is defended by the player's own calendar.

**[Ledger × REMEMBER] — The collection outlives the collector.** Lapsed players
keep paying for storage (literally: subscriptions maintained for years "so my stuff
isn't deleted"). The inventory is autobiography (#120), and deleting it is biography-
burning. Archive access — read-only visits to one's dead characters — is a product
nobody ships and everybody wants.

**[Eye × START] + [Eye × REMEMBER] — Beauty bookends.** The Eye rules the first ten
minutes (screenshots sell the start) and the memory (the vista is what remains), but
contributes almost nothing to STAY — mid-career players report not *seeing* the
environments anymore (semantic satiation of place). Beauty is a bookend motive;
budgeting art for the middle of the career is spending where no one is looking.

**[Animal × STAY] — The loop under the loops.** When all narrative and social layers
wear off, what remains in long-career games is paleolithic: forage, hoard, tend, chase
(Family J). Games with a sound animal-loop under their themed loops (Minecraft:
forage/nest; Warframe: hunt/hoard) achieve decade careers; games that are all Mask and
no Animal bounce off the Settling stage. **Theme is what starts careers; the animal
loop is what survives them.**

## Matrix II — Motive Families × This Repo's Prototypes

Hypotheses, not verdicts — each is a playtest question for the games in this monorepo.

**warrior** (roguelike combat, breakable-combo philosophy):
Primary: Will (#26 consequence-free power, #28 plateau-break) + Mind (#14 system
disassembly — the game-breaking gear hunt *is* A4's laboratory).
Matrix says: the breakable-combo stance is exactly right (D9 — feed the chaos-makers;
they're the canaries). Watch: [Mind × QUIT] — once the rule-space feels fully
disassembled, the happy graduation arrives; a new affix *family* is a fresh hypothesis
space and beats more content of known families.

**augur** (precog village, Madness core):
Primary: Abyss (#70 meaning-in-bounds, #75 the tended garden) + Weather (the
Madness/Trust economy is literally an emotion-regulation loop rendered as mechanics).
Matrix says: [Abyss × STAY] is augur's natural engine — the vale *needs* the seer;
being-needed (#57) should be made visceral (villagers who reference what your warnings
saved). Watch: A11's break-point — if neglect punishes too hard, gardening-dread
replaces gardening-peace.

**engarde** (status-ladder dueling):
Primary: Others (#48 status, #52 rivalry-as-relationship) + Eye (#94 style as content).
Matrix says: P13 applies — make results *testifiable* (a shareable bout summary is
testimony currency). [Will × RETURN]: a stinging, honorable loss is the retention
engine; ensure losses feel like *open loops* (rematch affordance) not closed doors.

**powerrange** (cannon/shield/range duel):
Primary: Mind (#19 thinking ahead, #15 hypothesis play) — a thin game (D10), aging
like mathematics if the core duel stays pure.
Matrix says: guard the thinness; grafts (Matrix I) should be *social* (testimony,
rivalry) not *content* (progression systems would thicken and spoil it). The knight-
capture windfall is Family C grace (#D7) — correctly rare.

**heal** (healer game):
Primary: Others (#50 cooperative intimacy, #57 being-needed) — healing is the
being-needed motive *as a verb*.
Matrix says: [Abyss × STAY] again — the healer's meaning is others' dependence; even
solo, simulate it (NPCs who survive *because of you*, and say so). Watch [Weather]:
healing under pressure is anxiety-shaped; ensure D2 arousal has a down-shifted mode
or the game competes for the smallest daypart.

**reclaimer**:
Primary (from the name and genre): Ledger (#116 cartographic compulsion, reclaiming
as tidying, D9 order-making) + Animal (forage/tend).
Matrix says: order-making games satisfy via *visible before/after*; the single
highest-leverage feature is a way to see how far the reclamation has come (the
cleared map as autobiography, #120).

## Harvest

- **Grafting is the master retention move** — longevity is serial transplantation of
  motive families, not deeper wells of the original motive.
- **Real motives leave stories; counterfeits leave elapsed time** — a falsifiable test
  for the Study 01 counterfeit thesis, usable in playtesting ("tell me about last
  session" → if no stories come, hooks are doing the retention).
- **Beauty bookends; the animal loop endures; meaning is the cheapest content** —
  a budget-allocation heuristic in nine words.
- Repo-wide: every prototype's strongest matrix cell is an **Others/Abyss** cell
  (being-needed, testimony, rivalry) — social-meaning motives — while all six games
  are currently solo-focused. The single biggest opportunity across the portfolio may
  be *lightweight testimony*: shareable artifacts of what happened (bout summaries,
  vale chronicles, cleared maps) that let solo play produce social objects.
