# WHY PLAY? — An Ideonomic Inquiry

An exploration of the question **"Why do people play a game — and why do they keep
playing?"** conducted in the spirit of Patrick Gunkel's *ideonomy*: the systematic,
combinatorial, deliberately exhaustive generation and organization of ideas.

Ideonomy's wager is that ideas have an anatomy. If you enumerate relentlessly,
divide along many axes at once, negate, analogize, permute, and cross lists against
lists, you surface ideas that no amount of staring at the question directly would
produce. The lists are not the product; the *collisions* are.

## Method

Each study applies one ideonomic organon (instrument) to the question:

| # | Study | Organon |
|---|-------|---------|
| 01 | [Organon of Motives](01-organon-of-motives.md) | **Enumeration** — the great list of reasons to play |
| 02 | [Dimensions of Play-Hunger](02-dimensions.md) | **Dimensional analysis** — axes along which the "why" varies |
| 03 | [Antitheses](03-antitheses.md) | **Negation** — why people don't play, and why they stop |
| 04 | [Paradoxes](04-paradoxes.md) | **Contradiction** — where play refuses to make sense |
| 05 | [Analogies](05-analogies.md) | **Analogy** — what play is *like*, and what each likeness reveals |
| 06 | [Transformations](06-transformations.md) | **Temporal morphology** — how the "why" mutates over a player's life with a game |
| 07 | [The Great Matrix](07-matrix.md) | **Combination** — motive families × temporal phases, crossed for sparks |
| 08 | [Synthesis](08-synthesis.md) | **Convergence** — prime-mover candidates and testable design maxims |

## Ground rules

- Prefer *many* middling ideas over few polished ones; polish comes at synthesis.
- Every list must be over-complete: include the obvious, the embarrassing, the dark,
  and the absurd. The absurd entries are bait for real ones.
- Negations and paradoxes are first-class evidence — a theory of play that cannot
  explain *quitting* explains nothing.
- Where possible, test findings against the live prototypes in this repo
  (warrior, augur, engarde, powerrange, heal, reclaimer).
