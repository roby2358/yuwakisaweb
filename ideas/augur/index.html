Pending Fates

Divine at the Shrine or the Standing Stones · warn at the Chapel or the Wan Hart

HOW TO STOP A DOOM 1. Divine at the Shrine or Stones until you know what and where. It is a gamble: the veil may hold, show what it pleases, or part for your choosing.
2. Walk there and press P — raise the ward that counters that doom.
3. Stack raises: each is ≈6 ward for 2 supplies and an action — and doing something eases your Burden.
4. Or Warn (Chapel / Wan Hart): the village adds aid every dawn — but the vigil drains Trust and halts the harvest. The village watches you, too: ward a doom you have named to yourself, and the more they trust you, the likelier they take up the vigil unasked — and a vigil they chose drains no Trust.
5. On the fated day, ward + aid meets the doom's weight. Enough, and it breaks. The matching ward is spent either way; wrong wards keep.

AUGUR

Your visions are always true and never complete. Something red-toothed is coming — but where? The mill is doomed — but when? You are the village augur, and the future arrives in facets: what, where, when, who, how hard.

Divine to know more, and carry the weight of knowing. Raise wards where you dare to guess. Warn the village and spend its patience. Fame will find you — and fame draws grander dooms. There is no ending and no defeat: dooms land, stones are raised, the vale endures, and so do you.
